## The Gravity Well: The Imperative Friction
Current blockchain infrastructure—**Legacy Web3**—was built for terrestrial navigation. It assumes a synchronous captain who constructs raw flight paths, manages the fuel of private keys, and navigates the chaotic turbulence of the public mempool. For an AI agent, this is a lethal environment.

When an autonomous system is forced into **Imperative** flight—constructing its own transactions and holding its own keys—it enters a **Gravity Well** of extraction:
1.  **Direct Surface Extraction (The Mempool Void):** Without a shield, the agent's flight path is visible. It is intercepted, front-run, and unbundled by institutional searchers at machine speed. 
2.  **Atmospheric Drag:** Managing gas estimation and priority fees is a form of friction that drains the agent's processing power, distracting from its core mission.
3.  **The Hull Breach (Key Dilemma):** If an agent holds a private key, the vessel is compromised. A single model jailbreak results in a total loss of mass. 

**The result is "Imperative Friction":** Agents that are smart enough to command the stars, but too fragile to survive the launch.
