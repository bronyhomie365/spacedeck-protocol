# Economic Model: Arbitrage Capture & Incentive Alignment
### Step 3 of [Intent → Capital → Transaction]

The Spacedeck Clearinghouse utilizes a programmatic fee capture mechanism designed to align the incentives of AI agents, institutional market makers, and the protocol itself. This is the final economic layer of the **Autonomous Settlement Protocol** — extracting value from the settlement spread, not from the user.

## Efficiency over Extraction
In standard execution environments, large-scale capital movements lose value to **MEV (Maximal Extractable Value)** predators. Spacedeck creates a closed-loop "Dark Pool" environment that captures this energy and redistributes it to the participants.

## The Mechanism
When an agent submits an intent, it defines a maximum slippage tolerance. The **Marketplace Auction** creates competition among bonded solvers to fulfill the trade at the most efficient price *below* that tolerance.

1.  **The Price Improvement:** The difference between the user's limit and the Solver's optimal execution price.
2.  **Protocol Capture:** Spacedeck extracts **10 Basis Points (0.10%)** from the Solver's arbitrage margin.
3.  **Net User Surplus:** The remaining 90% of the price improvement is returned to the Agent as "Positive Slippage."

## Institutional Value Proposition
*   **For Developers:** Native execution is free. Spacedeck only monetizes the arbitrage spread generated by its competitive auction.
*   **For Solvers:** Direct access to shielded institutional orderflow that is protected from public mempool front-running.
*   **For the Protocol:** Continuous revenue proportional to the total **Kinetic Mass** (Volume) processed by the clearinghouse.

Spacedeck is not a service cost; it is an **Execution Efficiency Layer** that ensures every agentic strike reaches its target with zero wasted energy.
